## 1. Loan File Receipt and Triage

### 1.1 Queue Assignment
Files enter the underwriting queue from Encompass in FIFO order with priority overrides:
- **Priority 1 (Rush)**: Rate lock expiring within 5 days, contractual close date within 7 days
- **Priority 2 (Standard)**: All other pipeline files
- **Priority 3 (Rework)**: Files returned from suspension

Average daily queue depth target: 8-12 files per underwriter.

### 1.2 Initial File Review (30-minute checklist)
Before beginning full underwrite, confirm:
- [ ] AUS findings printed and attached
- [ ] Borrower identity documents present
- [ ] 4506-C signed and submitted
- [ ] Credit report dated within 120 days
- [ ] OFAC/SAM screening completed
- [ ] All required disclosures delivered
- [ ] Processor checklist signed off

If any items missing, return to processor with SUSPEND status and condition list within 2 hours.

## 2. Credit Analysis

### 2.1 Credit Report Review
1. Verify representative score calculation (median of 3; lower of 2 borrowers)
2. Review tradeline history:
   - Minimum 3 active tradelines with 12+ month history
   - No 60+ day lates in most recent 12 months
   - No 90+ day lates in most recent 24 months (conventional)
3. Review public records:
   - Bankruptcy: 4yr seasoning (Ch7), 2yr seasoning (Ch13) from discharge
   - Foreclosure: 7yr seasoning from completion date
   - Short sale: 4yr seasoning
4. Document any credit inquiries in last 90 days with LOE if applicable
5. Validate liabilities against application disclosures

### 2.2 Debt-to-Income Calculation
**Front-end ratio** (housing): PITIA / Gross Monthly Income
**Back-end ratio** (total): (PITIA + all recurring obligations) / Gross Monthly Income

Include in total debt:
- Proposed PITIA (principal, interest, taxes, insurance, association dues)
- Installment debts with >10 payments remaining
- All revolving account minimum payments
- Student loans (use 1% of balance if IBR/deferred, per AUS)
- Child support/alimony (court-ordered)
- Car leases

Exclude:
- Debts being paid off at closing (with evidence)
- Business debts (verified via 2yr tax returns with business writes off)

### 2.3 AUS Reconciliation
Compare manual calculations to AUS output. Flag if:
- Manual DTI differs by >2% from AUS DTI
- AUS conditions not addressed in file
- Data entry errors in AUS submission

## 3. Income Analysis

### 3.1 Salaried Borrowers
1. Calculate base + guaranteed overtime/bonus (if 2yr history)
2. YTD earnings must be consistent with prior year
3. If income declining >10% YOY, use lower figure with documented justification
4. Employment gaps >30 days require written LOE

### 3.2 Self-Employed Borrowers
1. Average net income from 2 most recent tax years
2. Add back: depreciation, depletion, amortization, meals/entertainment (50%)
3. Subtract: non-recurring income items
4. If business shows declining revenue (>15% YOY), use most recent year only
5. Require CPA-prepared YTD profit and loss if >90 days from tax filing

### 3.3 Variable Income (Commission, Bonus, Overtime)
- Must have 2-year documented history
- Use 24-month average
- If trending downward, use 12-month average
- If <2yr history, cannot use for qualifying (unless base salary sufficient)

## 4. Asset Verification

### 4.1 Funds to Close
Calculate:
```
  Down payment
+ Closing costs (from LE/CD)
+ Prepaid items
- Seller credits
- Lender credits
= Total funds to close
```

Verify source of each dollar. Required reserves vary by product and occupancy.

### 4.2 Large Deposit Review
Any single deposit >50% of qualifying monthly income within 60-day statement period:
- Requires written LOE from borrower
- Paper trail (receipt, cancelled check, transfer record)
- If sourced from gift, full gift documentation required

## 5. Property Review

### 5.1 Appraisal Analysis
1. Verify subject property address and legal description match application
2. Review comparable sales:
   - Minimum 3 closed comparables within 12 months and 1 mile (urban) or 5 miles (rural)
   - Adjustments should not exceed 15% per line or 25% net
3. Check for adverse conditions:
   - Environmental hazards, easements, encroachments
   - Declining market indicators
   - Deferred maintenance items
4. If appraised value < purchase price, options:
   - Renegotiate price
   - Borrower brings difference to closing
   - Order reconsideration of value (with new comparables)
   - Deny/withdraw

### 5.2 Condo/PUD Review
Additional requirements per Fannie/Freddie guidelines:
- HOA questionnaire (budget, reserves, litigation, insurance)
- Owner-occupancy ratio minimum 50%
- No single entity owns >10% of units
- Insurance coverage meets minimum requirements

## 6. Decision and Conditions

### 6.1 Decision Types
| Decision | Definition |
|----------|-----------|
| **Approved** | All conditions satisfied; clear to close |
| **Approved with Conditions** | Approval contingent on prior-to-doc or prior-to-close items |
| **Suspended** | Material documentation missing; cannot make decision |
| **Denied** | Does not meet policy requirements; adverse action required |
| **Withdrawn** | Borrower request or abandonment (no contact 30+ days) |

### 6.2 Standard Conditions (Prior to Closing)
- Final VOE within 10 business days of closing
- Updated title with no new exceptions
- Hazard insurance binder with mortgagee clause
- Clear termite/pest inspection (if required by state)
- Executed purchase contract with all amendments

### 6.3 Condition Clearing
Processors clear conditions and submit to underwriter for sign-off. Underwriter must:
1. Review each cleared condition within 24 hours
2. Sign off or re-suspend with detailed explanation
3. Issue CTC (Clear to Close) only when ALL conditions satisfied
4. CTC must be issued by same underwriter who made original decision (continuity rule)

## 7. Tolerances and Exceptions

### 7.1 Guideline Exceptions
Any deviation from published product guidelines requires:
1. Written exception request from LO/processor with compensating factors
2. Approval per authority matrix (see Policy LO-POL-001 Section 8)
3. Documented in Exception Tracking System with unique exception ID
4. Post-close QC review guaranteed for all exception loans

### 7.2 Common Compensating Factors
- Significant liquid reserves (6+ months PITIA)
- Low LTV (<75%)
- Stable employment (5+ years same employer)
- Conservative DTI on one ratio if other is exceeded
- Minimal credit utilization (<30%)
- Substantial down payment from own funds
